What We Found After Testing: 6 Key Features

💨

Suction: 12,000Pa Handles What Other Robots Leave Behind

Most robot vacuums in this price range top out at 8,000Pa. The S5V's 12,000Pa HyperForce suction — powered by a brushless motor — makes a noticeable difference on low-pile carpets and hardwood gaps where debris embeds.

In our review of verified Amazon purchases, "cleaning power" was the second most-cited positive factor (246 mentions) after overall functionality (309 mentions).

⚠️ Honest caveat: For deep-pile carpets over 1 inch, no robot vacuum at this price fully replaces a traditional upright. The S5V is exceptional for daily maintenance cleaning.
🧹

Mopping: FlexiArm Is the Feature That Justifies the $50 Premium

Standard robot mop arms sit fixed inside the body — they cannot reach the last inch at walls and under furniture legs. The S5V's FlexiArm physically extends outward during mopping to cover those gaps. Paired with 200RPM dual spinning pads (vs drag-cloth designs), it removes dried spills most robots smear rather than clean.

The 10mm mop auto-lift on carpet detection is reliable — verified by dozens of Amazon reviewers who specifically tested this on mixed-floor homes.

⚠️ Honest caveat: Dried, caked-on kitchen grease still requires pre-treatment. The S5V is a daily/weekly maintenance mopper, not a deep-scrub replacement.
🏠

Self-Emptying Dock: 10 Weeks Hands-Free Is Not Marketing Hype

One Amazon reviewer (MalinoisLover, 40 upvotes) ran the S5V daily with two shedding dogs for over two months before the dust bag showed full — confirming the 10-week claim holds under heavy use.

The hot-air drying cycle (runs ~2 hours post-mop) prevents the mildew smell that plagues damp-dock competitors like eufy X10 Pro. This alone is a meaningful quality-of-life difference for households that run the mop function daily.

Auto water tank refilling means the robot handles 3,552 sq ft of mopping without you touching the water supply.

🗺️

Navigation: 4-Floor Mapping Matters More Than You Think

LiDAR-based mapping (vs camera-only cheaper alternatives) works in full darkness — important for scheduled overnight cleanings. The S5V builds and stores maps for up to 4 floor plans, auto-switching when it recognizes which level it's on.

The SmartPlan feature analyzes each room's floor type and automatically adjusts suction and mop intensity — you set it once and never touch it again.

For single-story homes under 2,000 sq ft, 4-floor mapping is overkill. For multi-story or large homes, it's the reason to choose S5V over Qrevo Series (3-floor limit).

🐾

Pet Hair: SGS-Certified 0% Tangle Rate — What That Actually Means

"SGS-certified" means an independent German testing lab (SGS Group) ran standardized hair-wrap tests and verified 0% tangling. This is not a marketing claim — it's a third-party certification.

In practice: the DuoDivide brush uses a split-channel design that shunts hair away from the axle rather than wrapping around it. Multiple Amazon reviewers with high-shedding breeds (German Shepherds, Huskies) confirm the brush stays clean between maintenance cycles.

⚠️ Honest caveat: Very long human hair (12"+) can still occasionally wrap if the brush isn't cleaned monthly. This is true of all current robot vacuum designs.
📱

App & Smart Home: Works Reliably, 2.4GHz Only

The Roborock app is consistently rated as one of the better robot vacuum apps — intuitive room-by-room control, cleaning history logs, and real-time map tracking. Alexa and Google Home voice control works reliably for basic commands (start cleaning, return to dock, clean specific room).

⚠️ Important technical note: The S5V supports 2.4GHz WiFi only. If your router broadcasts 5GHz only (rare but possible on some mesh systems), you'll need to enable a 2.4GHz band separately. Most routers broadcast both automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Qrevo CurvX is newer — should I get that instead?

The CurvX launched at $1,499.99 — nearly 3× the S5V's price. It offers 22,000Pa (vs 12,000Pa) and an ultra-slim 3.14" profile. For the vast majority of households, the performance difference does not justify a $950 premium.

The S5V covers every practical cleaning need at 37% of the CurvX's price. If budget is no object and you want the absolute latest flagship, the CurvX is fine. Otherwise, the S5V is the rational choice.

Will it drop to $499 again?

Unlikely in the near term. The $499 price occurred once — on Black Friday 2025 (Nov 24) — and lasted one day. Since then it has stabilized at $549–$580 for 3+ months. The next potential drop windows are Prime Day (July 2026) and Black Friday 2026, both 5–8 months away. If you need a robot vacuum now, $549 is as close to the floor as current data supports.

Will it wet my carpets while mopping?

No. The S5V automatically lifts mops 10mm when carpet is detected via floor sensor. This has been tested and confirmed by multiple Amazon reviewers on mixed-floor homes. Low-pile rugs (under ~0.4") are handled cleanly.

I have 2 large dogs — will hair jam the brushes?

This is exactly what the SGS-certified DuoDivide brush was designed for. MalinoisLover (40 upvotes) ran it daily with 2 shedding dogs for over 2 months without a jam. The split-channel design routes hair away from the axle. Monthly brush check is still recommended for long-haired breeds, but daily emergency cleanouts are not required.

Does it need 5GHz WiFi?

No — it requires 2.4GHz only. Most home routers broadcast both bands. If you have a mesh system that uses band-steering, you may need to split your 2.4GHz and 5GHz into separate network names temporarily during setup. One-time process, takes 5 minutes.
