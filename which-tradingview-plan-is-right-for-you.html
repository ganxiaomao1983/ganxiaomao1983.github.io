Diagnostic

Do you need a paid plan?

TradingView's free Basic plan is genuinely functional, not just a preview. Before comparing trading view plans and pricing in detail, most users should only upgrade once a specific limit gets in the way of a workflow they actually use.

  • 1Are you hitting the 3-alert limit on the free plan on a regular basis?
  • 2Do you need more than 2 indicators on a single chart, or more than 1 chart per tab?
  • 3Would an ad-free workspace meaningfully improve your day-to-day use?
  • 4Do you need webhook notifications to connect alerts to a bot or automation service?
  • 5Are expiring alerts (roughly every two months on Essential and Plus) a maintenance problem for your strategy?
  • 6Do you need second-based alerts, tick data, or deep backtesting for a high-frequency or institutional workflow?

If you answered "no" to all of these, the free Basic plan is likely sufficient. If you answered "yes" to one or more, the sections below map each answer to a specific plan.

Plan fit

Which plan fits your workflow?

Third-party reviewers consistently map plan recommendations to how a person actually uses the platform, not to a single "best plan" answer.

Casual learner / light investor

Checks charts occasionally, uses one or two indicators, and does not rely on alerts as part of an active trading process.

Fits: Basic or Essential

Best fit for traders who need more alerts and layouts than the free plan offers, but nothing beyond that. If you have not hit any Basic-plan limits, staying free is a reasonable choice.

  • Upgrade trigger: ads become disruptive to daily use.
  • Upgrade trigger: you start hitting the 3-alert limit.

Active day trader or swing trader

Runs multiple chart layouts daily, monitors several instruments at once, and depends on alerts for trade signals.

Often chosen: Plus

Often chosen by active traders who have outgrown the free plan. Plus supports 4 charts per tab, 100 alerts, and priority support.

  • Upgrade trigger: Essential's 20-alert limit feels restrictive.
  • Upgrade trigger: you need to monitor 4 instruments at once.

Algorithmic trader / automation user

Connects alerts to webhooks that trigger bots or third-party execution services. Alert reliability and expiry behavior are the deciding factors.

Fits: Essential to start, Premium for scale

Essential includes webhook support and 20 alerts, sufficient for many starting strategies. Premium is recommended once expiring alerts (roughly every two months on Essential and Plus) become a maintenance burden, since Premium alerts never expire.

  • Upgrade trigger: manually resetting expired alerts every two months.
  • Upgrade trigger: your strategy needs second-based alerts or deep backtesting.

Professional / institutional / prop firm user

Operates at high capacity with many concurrent alerts, tick-based data needs, and multi-monitor setups.

Fits: Premium, Ultimate for professional accounts

Premium provides sufficient capacity for most individual traders, even serious ones. Ultimate is only justified for institutional users, prop firms, or accounts formally classified as professional by TradingView.

  • Upgrade trigger: you qualify as a professional user under TradingView's classification.
  • Upgrade trigger: you need tick-based data or over 400 concurrent alerts.

Subscription Cost, Billing & Trials Explained

Your subscription price and your market data are billed separately. Understanding both, plus how trials and cancellations work, avoids billing surprises.

Free trial details

Essential, Plus, and Premium include a 30-day free trial. Ultimate includes a 14-day trial only. Payment details are required to start a trial, and you will not be charged until the trial ends.

  1. Go to tradingview.com/pricing and select a plan.
  2. Click the trial button for that plan and enter payment details.
  3. Note your trial end date.
  4. Cancel via Account and Billing before the trial ends if you do not want to continue.
Cancel at least 24 hours before your renewal or trial end date to avoid being charged for the next billing period. TradingView does not typically issue refunds for subscriptions already billed.

Canceling a subscription

  1. Log into your TradingView account.
  2. Click your profile picture and select Account and Billing.
  3. Go to the Subscriptions tab under Billing.
  4. Select Cancel renewal or Cancel subscription and confirm.
  5. You retain paid features until the end of the current billing period.

Paying via PayPal may offer an easier cancellation path, since PayPal subscriptions can be managed directly through the PayPal interface.

Plan features vs. market data costs

Your plan controls platform features like charts, indicators, and alerts. Real-time market data from specific exchanges is a separate cost.

  • Free on all plans: crypto data, major forex pairs, and delayed (~15 min) stock data.
  • Extra cost: real-time US stock data (~$9.95/month) and real-time CME futures data (~$13/month).
  • Mobile in-app purchases may cost more than web purchases due to app store platform fees.

Example: an active trader on Plus with US stock and crypto data pays roughly $29.95/month for the plan plus $9.95/month for stock data, about $40/month total.

Questions answered

Common Questions About TradingView Pricing, Cost & Subscriptions

Isn't TradingView's pricing too high for casual users?

This is a fair concern for genuinely light users. If you have not hit any limits on the free Basic plan, upgrading is not necessary — the free plan is fully functional for casual use. When you do look at trading view pricing across tiers, Essential at $12.95/month on annual billing is the lowest-cost paid option, and the right plan depends on your actual usage, not on what serious traders are assumed to need.

Does the same price mean the same value everywhere?

No. Subscription pricing represents different real value depending on region and income level, a documented and widely discussed structural issue. Annual billing and seasonal sales, particularly Black Friday, can substantially reduce the effective cost. Always confirm current pricing and any active promotions at tradingview.com/pricing.

Has TradingView raised prices over time?

Pricing has changed over time, which is a legitimate concern for long-term budget planning. Annual billing locks in the current price for one subscription year, and purchasing during major sale events is the most effective documented way to reduce long-term cost. Always verify the current price at tradingview.com/pricing before subscribing or renewing.

Is the free plan actually enough for most people?

For many users, yes. The free Basic plan is not just a trial — it includes functional charting, community indicators, paper trading, screeners, and brokerage integration. The honest recommendation is to upgrade only once the free plan actively constrains something in your real workflow.

Why am I being charged extra for market data if I already pay for a plan?

Your plan and real-time market data are billed separately. Crypto and forex real-time data are free on every plan, including Basic. Real-time US stock and futures data cost extra regardless of plan tier. Factor both costs into your budget before subscribing.

How hard is it to get a refund after being charged?

TradingView's refund policy is strict, and refunds are not routinely issued once a charge has processed. Protect yourself by canceling before your billing date, noting your exact renewal date, and considering PayPal for an easier independent cancellation path. Contact TradingView support directly before filing a bank chargeback, since direct contact may resolve the issue faster.
